About the Area

The Navigli District is one of Milan’s most atmospheric and vibrant areas, famous for its historic canals, charming bridges, lively nightlife, and romantic aperitivo spots. Originally built to transport goods (and even stones for the Duomo!), the canals are now lined with cafés, restaurants, art galleries, and vintage markets.

🚇 How to get there:
Take metro M2 (green line) and stop at Porta Genova.
You can also walk from the Darsena or use tram lines 2, 3 and 9.

1. Naviglio Grande

The main canal of the district, flanked by cobbled streets, art studios, cocktail bars, and trattorie.
A perfect place for sunset walks, photos, and aperitivi.

2. Vicolo dei Lavandai

📍 Open in Google Maps
A narrow alley next to the Naviglio where women once came to wash clothes. It’s one of the most picturesque and historic corners in Milan, preserved with original wooden washbasins and covered walkways.

3. Darsena

📍 Open in Google Maps
The dock area where the Naviglio Grande and Pavese meet. A wide open space with benches, food stalls, water views, and frequent flea markets. Very lively on weekends.

About the Area

The “Quadrilatero della Moda” (Fashion Quadrilateral) is Milan’s luxury fashion district, known for its high-end boutiques, elegant streets, and glamorous atmosphere. It's one of the most famous fashion areas in the world.

🛍️ Streets like Via Montenapoleone, Via della Spiga, Via Manzoni, and Via Sant’Andrea are home to iconic fashion houses including Gucci, Prada, Chanel, Versace, and many more.

🚇 How to get there:
Take Metro M3 (yellow line) and stop at Montenapoleone.
Alternatively, walk from San Babila (M1 red line).

1. Via Montenapoleone

The most prestigious shopping street in Milan. Luxury brands, fashion flagships, elegant people watching and refined window displays.
📍 Open in Google Maps

2. Via della Spiga

A pedestrian-only cobbled street with beautifully decorated boutiques and showrooms.
📍 Open in Google Maps

3. Gallerie d’Italia – Milano

Elegant art museum located in a historic palazzo, with a refined permanent collection and rotating exhibitions.
📍 Piazza della Scala — Open in Google Maps

4. Palazzo Morando

A quiet museum dedicated to Milanese fashion, style and urban history.
📍 Via Sant’Andrea 6 — Open in Google Maps
🆓 Free entrance

5. Via Manzoni

This elegant street starts right after Piazza della Scala and leads to the Porta Nuova arch. It's lined with luxury hotels, haute couture boutiques, and refined restaurants. It’s named after the famous Milanese novelist Alessandro Manzoni.
📍 Open in Google Maps

6. ☕🍽️ Cafés & Fine Dining in the District

  • ☕ Cova Montenapoleone – Via Montenapoleone 8
    Historic pasticceria and café, elegant and timeless
  • 🍽️ Bice Milano – Via Borgospesso 12
    Iconic Milanese restaurant serving traditional dishes since 1926
  • 🍷 Il Salumaio di Montenapoleone – Via Santo Spirito 10
    Gourmet dining with a beautiful historic courtyard

⚠️ Before entering cafés or restaurants, we recommend checking prices first.

About the Area

Porta Venezia and Palestro are elegant districts that mix culture, green spaces, liberty-style architecture, and lively nightlife. It’s ideal for families during the day and for aperitivo lovers in the evening.

🚇 How to get there:
Metro Line M1 (Red): Porta Venezia or Palestro

1. Indro Montanelli Public Gardens

🌳 One of the largest green areas in central Milan, perfect for relaxing, walking, or picnics.
It also hosts several cultural institutions and statues.

2. Museo Civico di Storia Naturale

🦖 Museum of Natural History – dinosaurs, minerals, animals and more.
🎟️ Great for kids and families.
📍 Open in Google Maps

3. Planetario di Milano

🪐 The city’s planetarium – shows, projections and astronomy events in a neoclassical dome.
📍 Open in Google Maps

4. GAM – Galleria d’Arte Moderna

🖼️ 19th- and early 20th-century Italian art housed in Villa Reale.
📍 Open in Google Maps

5. Corso Buenos Aires

🛍️ One of the longest shopping streets in Europe – popular fashion chains, fast fashion, shoes, and accessories.
Very busy, especially during sales periods.

6. Bar Basso – Negroni Sbagliato Origin

🍸 A true Milanese institution where the Negroni Sbagliato cocktail was invented.
Located at the end of Via Plinio, easily reachable on foot from M1 Lima.
📍 Via Plinio 39 – Open in Google Maps

7. Via Lecco – LGBTQIA+ Friendly Area

🌈 Vibrant street near Porta Venezia filled with inclusive bars, cafés and nightlife venues.
A reference point for Milan’s LGBTQIA+ community, especially during Milano Pride.

8. Casa Galimberti – Art Nouveau Jewel

🏛️ Stunning example of Milanese liberty architecture with colorful tiles and wrought iron balconies.
📍 Via Marcello Malpighi 3 – Open in Google Maps
