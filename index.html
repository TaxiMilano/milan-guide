8. Santuario di San Bernardino alle Ossa

📍 Piazza Santo Stefano, Milan

🕍 A unique Catholic sanctuary famous for its ossuary – a small chapel decorated with human skulls and bones, built in the 13th century.

⏰ Opening Hours:
– Monday to Friday: 8:00 – 18:00
– Saturday: 9:30 – 18:00
– Sunday: 9:30 – 12:00
The ossuary is closed on Sundays but otherwise follows the same hours.

🆓 Free entry. Donations are welcome. Please note this is a place of worship, not a museum – respectful behavior is required during visits.

📍 Via degli Omenoni 3, Milan

🏛️ A historic Milanese palace dating back to the 16th century. Though extensively renovated over the centuries, the sculpted facade remains nearly untouched. The building can be admired from the outside for free. Entrance is restricted to members of the exclusive Milanese club "Il Clubino".

About Brera

Brera is Milan’s bohemian and artistic neighborhood, filled with charm, cobbled streets, art galleries, boutique shops and cozy cafés. It’s a perfect area for a cultural walk and a relaxed aperitivo. The heart of Brera is bordered by Via Pontaccio, Via Fatebenefratelli, Via Borgonuovo, Via Monte di Pietà, Via dell’Orso, Via Ponte Vetero, and Via Mercato.

1. Pinacoteca di Brera

📍 Via Brera 28, Milan

🖼️ One of Italy’s most important art galleries, home to masterpieces by Caravaggio, Raphael, Mantegna, Tintoretto, and the iconic painting The Kiss by Hayez.

⏰ Opening Hours: Tuesday – Sunday, 8:30am – 7:15pm (last entrance at 6pm)
❌ Closed: Mondays and December 25th
🆓 Free admission (with reservation) on the first Sunday of each month

2. Brera Botanical Garden

📍 Via Fiori Oscuri 4, Milan (Entrances also from Via Brera 28 and Via Privata Fratelli Gabba 10)

🌿 A peaceful green oasis in the heart of Brera, offering a quiet retreat among medicinal plants, ancient trees and floral displays.

🚇 Easily reachable on foot from the metro stops Montenapoleone, Lanza or Cairoli.

ℹ️ More info: Visit the Garden – Official Site

About the Area

This neighborhood is one of Milan’s most iconic and beloved areas, where history, culture, and nature blend beautifully. Dominated by the grand Renaissance fortress of the Castello Sforzesco—built in the 15th century by Francesco Sforza—it now houses several important museums and priceless works of art, including Michelangelo’s final sculpture, the Pietà Rondanini.

Behind the castle lies Parco Sempione, a large landscaped park created in the late 19th century. It offers open lawns, centuries-old trees, a scenic lake, and several cultural landmarks like the Arena Civica, the Triennale Museum, and Torre Branca with its panoramic city views.

1. Castello Sforzesco

🏰 A majestic Renaissance castle housing multiple civic museums, historic courtyards, and exhibitions. Don’t miss Michelangelo’s Pietà Rondanini.

🎟️ Ticket required for museum access. Free access to the courtyards.

2. Parco Sempione

🌳 Milan’s most central park, ideal for walking, relaxing, or having a picnic. Includes lakes, playgrounds, sculptures, and quiet paths.

3. Arco della Pace

🕊️ The “Arch of Peace” marks the northern entrance to the park. Built in the 19th century, it’s a popular evening meeting point surrounded by bars and aperitivo spots.

4. Acquario Civico

🐠 One of the oldest aquariums in Europe. Small but charming, and great for families. Entry is free.

5. Torre Branca

🔭 A 108-meter-high metal tower inside the park. Take the elevator to the top for one of the best panoramic views of Milan.
